If you put 20% down, you do not have to pay Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI).
If however, like most of us, you don't put 20% down, you are required to pay PMI.


If you have an FHA loan, you're bound to pay this PMI monthly payment until the end of your loan. That's a lot of money = PMI * 360 (for a regular 30 year loan).

If you have regular mortgage (which I'd recommend), you're not responsible for PMI after owning 22% equity. For the average person this takes 7 years to achieve. Do you want to be paying PMI for 7 years?? NOPE

Scroll down the Amortization table to find out when your scenario would reach 22% equity.
